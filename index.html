How does it work-?

Paige runs on machine learning and gets information from open source material.
She's a Book-a-holic herself, constantly reading up and trying to help you find the best book for you

Phase 1: Gathering Knowledge

Paige thrives on publicly available information-!

She explores the vast world of open-source data to learn and grow. This means she focuses on information that's freely accessible online, respecting copyright and user privacy.Just like reading a library book, Paige can't access private information or copyrighted content without permission. Â She also avoids overwhelming websites with requests that might disrupt their normal operations.

Phase 2: Model Building    

 This is where the magic happens!  

In Phase 2, we build a machine learning model for Paige. Imagine this model as a special set of instructions that helps Paige analyze books and understand their content. We train Paige by feeding her a lot of data about books, including summaries, genres, reviews, and author information. By processing this data, Paige learns to identify patterns and relationships that will allow her to recommend books you'll love.
